Fun Facts

Exposure times in the old days

One popular photography technique invented in the 1830s was the Daguerreotype, where photographs were captured on silver coated plates. They required a lot of light and that meant long exposure times - sometimes as much as five minutes. No wonder the kids of those days sometimes look like life’s no fun at all.

BOKEH

The word bokeh refers to the way a lens handles unfocused light. It is a Japanese word meaning unsharp and many photographers will tell you their version of how it is pronounced. Native Japanese speakers will pronounce it “boh-keh” (/boke/ phonetically).
